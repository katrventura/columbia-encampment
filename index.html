A Timeline of the 14-Day of Gaza Solidarity Encampment in Columbia University

By Katrina Ventura

In the early morning of April 17, 2024 the Columbia community woke up to find green and blue tents pitched on the South lawn of the university. The Gaza Solidarity Encampment, as student demonstrators called it, acted as a space in the middle of Morningside campus where pro-Palestine students pressured the university to divest from companies that profit from the Israeli occupation of Gaza.

For 14 days, the liberation zone stood as dialogues between students and administrators took place. More than 100 students had been arrested after Columbia University called in the police to empty the encampment. On April 30, student demonstrators and police had its final standoff at Hamilton Hall, the same building occupied during the 1968 anti-Vietnam war protests.

This data project provides a timeline of the movement on campus during those 14 days.

Day 1: Hundreds of students set up tents on South Lawn beginning at around 4 a.m. on Wednesday morning, pledging to occupy the space until the University divests from companies with ties to Israel.

Day 2: Over 100 individuals arrested by police after University President Minouche Shafik authorized a sweep the “Gaza Solidarity Encampment” on South Lawn at around 1 p.m. on Thursday. The encampment was moved to the opposite lawn after the sweep.

Day 3: Protesters had tents pitched as of early Friday morning, but were told by Public Safety to remove them and primarily sat on blankets spread across the lawn.

Day 4: The encampment remained on South Lawn two days after the police sweep, all individuals had been released from custody as of late Thursday evening.

Day 5: Students stationed along the perimeter of the lawn fence facing Lerner Hall intercepted admitted students and their parents as they passed by, asking them to join the encampment. Two signs hung on the north end of the lawn read “Admitted Students Enroll in Revolution.” Student demonstrators have slept on tarps for three nights on South Lawn without over-head cover.

Day 6: After three nights, student demonstrators erected tents on South Lawn for the first time since the police sweep.

Day 7: The University opted to hold classes virtually as student demonstrators remained on South Lawn. At around 2 p.m., hundreds of people participated in a faculty walkout in front of the University Low Steps and calling for the University to grant amnesty to student protesters and remove the interim demonstration policy, which established designated times and places for on-campus protests.

Day 8: Columbia University President Minouche Shafik sent an email at 9:41 p.m. on Tuesday announcing a midnight deadline for negotiations with organizers of the “Gaza Solidarity Encampment,” before considering “alternative options” to clear South Lawn.

Day 9: In a 4:08 a.m. email to the Columbia community, the University announced that it would continue conversations with student representatives for the next 48 hours after they made four commitments. These include removing a significant number of tents from the lawn, ensuring that only Columbia affiliates will remain in the encampment, complying with all New York Fire Department requirements, and taking steps to “make the encampment welcome to all” and to prohibit “discriminatory or harassing language.”.

Day 10: Protesters joining the ‘United for Israel March’ head to the 116th Street and Amsterdam Avenue gates at around 6:30 pm on April 26, Friday.

Day 11: At the Morningside campus, CUID-only access remains in place until circumstances allow for broader access.

Day 12: At the Morningside campus, CUID-only access remains in place until circumstances allow for broader access.

Day 13: Columbia set a deadline for pro-Palestine protesters to vacate the encampment by 2 pm on Monday. Students have formed a picket line around the encampment.

Day 14: Student protestors occupied Hamilton Hall early Tuesday morning, the same building occupied during the 1968 anti-Vietnam war protests. Tents have been set up in front of Hamilton Hall. Hundreds of NYPD officers swarmed campus on Tuesday evening, arresting dozens of protesters both inside and outside of Hamilton. Columbia personnel disassembled the “Gaza Solidarity Encampment” at around 11:40 p.m. on Tuesday following the New York Police Department’s sweep of occupied Hamilton Hall. At 12:18 a.m. on Wednesday, the encampment was partially cleared.
